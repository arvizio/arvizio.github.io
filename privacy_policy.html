What information does Arvizio collect?

User accounts, registration, purchases, contacting us for support

When you register to use an Arvizio app or service, create an Arvizio user, purchase Arvizio product or contact us for support or other inquiries, Arvizio collects information that can identify you. This may include your name, company name, email address, or payment information you have provided to us to fulfill an order.  We may combine this information with information we collect from publicly available sources and business partners. 

Arvizio apps, websites and online services

We may collect information about how you use our apps and websites. We may collect information sent by your browser or device to our servers whenever you visit an Arvizio website or use an Arvizio app or services. For example, your browser or device may tell us your IP address (which may tell us generally where you are located) and the type of browser and device you used. When you visit an Arvizio website, your browser may also tell us information such as the page that led you to our website and, if applicable, the search terms you typed into a search engine that led you to our website. Arvizio may collect information about how you use our websites and apps by using cookies and similar technologies, and our servers may collect similar information when you are logged in to the app or website.

Arvizio app activation and automatic updates

When you activate your Arvizio app or when you install updates to the app, we may  collect certain information about your device (including your IP address). Some of our apps include features that connect to our servers and offer you the ability to install updates to the app. We may keep track of information such as whether the update was successful or not. We may use the information collected through activation or app updates to validate your copy of the app and confirm that it is properly licensed.

When you use our Services, we may place a text file called a “cookie” in the browser directory of your computer’s hard drive. Aggregate, but not individual, cookie and tracking information may be shared with third parties. Learn more about our Cookie Policy.
